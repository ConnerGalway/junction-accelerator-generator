## **Tactic 1: The Past-Client Engine — Email Automation, Reviews & Referrals**

**Phase:**  Foundation / Month 1

**Sidebar Label:**  Email & Referrals

**Subtitle:**  Connect your Zoho CRM to Mailchimp and automate the follow-up, reviews, and referral nudges you don’t have time to do by hand.

### **What We Heard From You**

You told us your single best source of business is couples referred by friends who eloped with you, and yet there’s no system capturing it. You have every couple’s email in Zoho but use maybe 1% of it, tried a newsletter once and let it go because you didn’t know what to say, and out of a hundred weddings you get tagged on Instagram maybe five times. Reviews are the same story: Google reviews matter most to you, but you only ask when a couple happens to gush at the end, and even then not consistently. The follow-up isn’t happening because you’re buried in delivery during the season. This is the type of repetitive, high-value work that should run on its own.

### **What This Is**

A simple automation layer on top of the tools you already pay for. You’ll connect your Zoho CRM to Mailchimp (which is free up to 250 contacts and far better for design than Zoho’s built-in email) so every couple flows in automatically. Then you’ll build one automated email that fires after each wedding to say thank you, ask for a Google review, and invite the couple to tag you on Instagram, triggered simply by moving the wedding to a “Complete” stage in Zoho. Finally, you'll set up two designed newsletters a year, one before the season, one after, built from photos you already have, plus a small referral perk for couples sent by past clients. Nothing here requires that you write from scratch each time.

### **Why It Matters**

This comes first because it’s the fastest, cheapest revenue you have, and it reduces the dependence on Google Ads you said you want out of. A referred couple is far more likely to book than a cold lead, so every extra review, tag, and forwarded newsletter compounds into bookings that cost you nothing in ad spend. It’s also the work least dependent on anyone else. It doesn’t require a developer, or contractor, so you can have it running within a few weeks. Skip it, and you keep paying to acquire strangers while your warmest audience stays untapped.

### **Implementation Steps**

#### ***Part A: Connect Your Tools***

1. **Create a free Mailchimp account and import your list.**

* Sign up at mailchimp.com using your contact@elopetofino.com address; the free plan covers up to 2,000 contacts, which is plenty to start.

* Export your contacts from Zoho (Contacts → Export) as a CSV and import them into Mailchimp under Audience → Import Contacts.

* Tag imported couples as “Past Client” so you can email married couples separately from enquiries later.

2. **Connect Zoho CRM to Mailchimp so couples are added and tagged automatically.**

* The goal in plain terms: when you drag a wedding to “Wedding Complete” on your Zoho board (Step 3), an automation adds a label called a “tag” — “Wedding Complete” — to that couple in Mailchimp. That tag is the on-switch for the thank-you-and-review email you build in Step 4; nothing sends until the tag is applied.

* *Note: At the bottom of this section we’ve provided a simpler, free option that will send less visually appealing emails, but will get the job done.*

* Check three prerequisites first, because they decide whether this works: (1) Zoho CRM is a “premium” app on Zapier, so you need a paid Zapier plan, the free Zapier tier cannot connect Zoho; (2) API access must be enabled on your Zoho account, and a paid Zoho edition is recommended for reliable, instant triggering. Confirm your edition or test before you rely on it; (3) your Mailchimp audience must use single opt-in, not double opt-in, or newly added couples won’t be taggable until they click a confirmation email. Make (make.com) is a lower-cost alternative to Zapier and works the same way, but it has the same Zoho API requirement.

* Build the trigger: in Zapier, click Create → Zap. Trigger app \= Zoho CRM; event \= “New/Updated Module Entry” (use the Instant version if your Zoho plan supports it); connect your Zoho account; set Module \= Deals.

* Add a Filter (“Filter by Zapier”) set to continue only when the deal’s Stage field exactly equals “Wedding Complete.” Without this, the Zap fires on every small edit to any deal.

* Look up the couple’s email (skip this only if you store the couple’s email directly on the deal record.). In Zoho CRM the email lives on the Contact, not the Deal, so add a Zoho CRM “Find Module Entry” action set to the Contacts module to find the contact linked to the deal and return their email address. 

* Add the couple to your audience: action \= Mailchimp “Add/Update Subscriber.” Choose your audience, map the email from the Find-Contact step, and set “Update Existing” to Yes so it also works for couples already imported in Step 1\. This must come before the tag step — the tag action fails if the email isn’t already in the audience.

* Apply the tag: add a second Mailchimp action, “Add Subscriber to Tag.” Select the same audience, map the same email, and set the tag to exactly “Wedding Complete” (you reuse this exact spelling in Step 4). If this step errors that the email “isn’t subscribed,” insert a short Delay step of a minute or two between the two Mailchimp actions.

* Turn the Zap on and test it end to end: drag a test wedding to “Wedding Complete” in Zoho and confirm the contact gains the “Wedding Complete” tag in Mailchimp and enters the Step 4 automation instantly on a paid Zoho plan, or within a few minutes on a polling trigger.

* Leave the tag in place after sending. Do not add a “remove tag” step. Because each couple marries once, the flow should run exactly once; Mailchimp only triggers when the tag is first added, so it won’t re-send even if the Zap touches the deal again later.

  * 

3. **Set up a “Wedding Complete” stage in your Zoho pipeline.**

* In your deals/pipeline view, add a stage called “Wedding Complete” after your final delivery stage if one doesn’t already exist. Do this before building the Zap in Step 2, since the Zap watches for this exact stage.

* Spell it identically everywhere — the Zoho stage, the Zapier filter, and the Mailchimp tag should all read “Wedding Complete” — so the three line up and the automation fires.

* This single drag-to-the-next-column action is what triggers everything downstream — keep it simple so it becomes part of your normal wrap-up routine after each wedding.

#### ***Part B: Automate the Post-Wedding Follow-Up***

4. **Build the post-wedding thank-you and review email in Mailchimp.**

* In Mailchimp, go to Automations and create a flow, then choose the “Tag added” starting point and select the “Wedding Complete” tag your Zap applies. Leave the default so each contact enters once..

* Write one warm, personal email: thank them, then make a single clear ask: a Google review, framed around supporting a small local business, the approach that already works for you.

* Include the direct Google review link (see Resources) as one obvious button, plus a soft line inviting them to tag @elope\_tofino in their photos.

5. **Set the email to send about one week after the wedding.**

* Add a delay so the email lands roughly seven days after the wedding date, not the day after,  couples are still travelling or decompressing immediately after.

* Turn the automation on and run yourself through it with a test contact to confirm timing, links, and formatting before it goes live.

6. **Add a light review-response routine.**

* Once a week, open your Google Business Profile and reply to every new review, positive or negative. Respond to all of them, not just the glowing ones.

* For a rare unhappy review, reply briefly, kindly, and without defensiveness; future couples judge you more on the response than the complaint.

* Keep using ChatGPT to draft replies, but add one specific detail from the wedding so it doesn’t read as canned.

**Basic alternative (Zoho-only, no Mailchimp integration)**:

* **Create the email first in Zoho.** Go to Setup → Templates → Email Templates and build the post-wedding email on the Deals (or Contacts) module: the thank-you, the single Google-review ask as a button, and the soft "tag @elope\_tofino" line. This is the same copy you'd have used in Mailchimp.  
* **Create the workflow rule.** Setup → Automation → Workflow Rules → Create Rule → module **Deals**. For the trigger, choose **Record Action → Edit**, and set it to fire only when a **specific field is modified: Stage**. Use an On Edit workflow rule on the Deals module when the Stage field is modified — and add the condition **Stage \= "Wedding Complete"** so it only fires at the final stage, not on every edit.   
* **Delay it \~7 days.** Instead of an instant action, attach a **scheduled (time-based) action**. You can schedule the actions to be triggered based on the Rule Trigger Time, so set it to \+7 days after the rule fires. (Even better: anchor it to your Wedding Date field, Zoho can also execute based on a date field's value, so it always lands a week after the actual wedding, not a week after you happened to update the record.)   
* **Attach the email as the action.** Create an **Email Notification** action, point it at the template from step 1, and associate it with that scheduled action. Browse for and associate an existing email template from the module's template folder, and set your From/Reply-to address.

#### ***Part C: The Twice-a-Season Newsletter and Referral Perk***

1. **Build a reusable newsletter template in Mailchimp.**

* Use Mailchimp’s template gallery. Search “wedding” for a designed layout so it looks professionally made without design work.

* Save it as a template you reuse each season by swapping photos and a short, personal note from you (around 200 words).

* The subject line matters more than anything else in the email. Write it to make a past couple want to open and reminisce, since that’s what gets it forwarded to engaged friends.

2. **Send the pre-season newsletter timed to the booking window.**

* Send the first newsletter in February or March, when couples are actively planning, so past clients forward it to friends at the moment those friends are deciding.

* Send it to the whole list; lead with beautiful imagery and what’s new this season (the all-inclusive retreat, the Ucluelet venue, the France option), not a hard sell.

3. **Send the end-of-season newsletter and launch a referral perk.**

* After the season, send a “thank you for an incredible season” newsletter with highlights. It’s a warm touchpoint with everyone who married with you that year.

* Introduce a simple, no-discount referral perk: couples referred by a past client receive a welcome touch (a small welcome basket or a bottle on arrival) rather than money off, protecting your premium positioning.

* Mention the perk in the newsletter and in your post-wedding email so past couples know there’s a reason to send friends your way.

## **Tactic 2: Website Foundation & Findability — Technical SEO, Resource Pages & AI Discoverability**

**Phase:**  Findability / Months 1–2

**Sidebar Label:**  Search & AI

**Subtitle:**  Clear the technical issues holding your site back, publish evergreen resource and FAQ pages, and get found in Google and AI search the way your competitors already are.

### **What We Heard From You**

You noticed something that stuck with you: when you search ChatGPT for an elopement planner in Tofino, your competitors come up and you don’t. Even a luxury-picnic company that started after you is appearing. Your business runs almost entirely on Google Ads, and you’re tired of the bidding war; you want your presence to be “more organic and less paid.” Your digital assessment found a strong foundation but eighteen fixable issues, and the most important ones: a missing sitemap, a too-short page title, missing image alt text, an oversized homepage, limit how well you rank. You also liked the idea of evergreen resource pages over a dated blog, and you have a coordinator with a marketing background ready to help write them.

### **What This Is**

This is the search-and-discovery side of the business, in three layers. First, a short technical brief you hand to the developer who already manages your custom site, fixing the high-priority items from your assessment so Google and AI tools can read and rank your pages properly. Second, a small library of evergreen resource pages and a proper FAQ page, the kind of plain, helpful content that answers what couples actually Google, and that AI tools like ChatGPT prefer to pull from. Third, the off-site work that builds authority: getting listed in free wedding directories and on Apple Business Profile, and earning links from the vendors you already work with. Your coordinator can own much of the writing; you mostly direct and approve.

### **Why It Matters**

Paid ads stop the moment you stop paying; this is the asset that keeps working for years. The technical fixes are foundational. There’s little point driving traffic to a site Google struggles to index, and AI tools won’t recommend a business they can’t clearly read. Evergreen pages are how a competitor who started after you ends up in ChatGPT ahead of you: they’ve given the AI something to quote, and you haven’t yet. Done now, before peak season, this lowers your reliance on Google Ads exactly as you asked, and the resource pages double as link bait for vendor partnerships and as material for the social content in Tactic 3\.

### **Implementation Steps**

#### ***Part A: Brief Your Developer on the Technical Fixes***

1. **Send your developer the high-priority SEO brief.**

* Forward the prioritised list and ask them to confirm what they can complete and by when; you only need the high-priority items first, not all eighteen.

* High priority: add an XML sitemap and submit it in Google Search Console; reduce the homepage to under 5MB (it’s currently 5.02MB); lengthen the page title from 27 characters to a 50–60 character keyword-rich title (e.g. “Tofino & Ucluelet Elopements & Micro Weddings | Elope Tofino”); expand the meta description to 120–160 characters; add descriptive alt text to all images (19 of 20 are missing it).

* Ask them to distribute your main keywords: Tofino, Ucluelet, elopement, micro wedding across page titles, headers, and alt text, not just the homepage.

2. **Have your developer add the AI and local-search items.**

* Add an llms.txt file so AI tools like ChatGPT and Gemini can read and represent your business accurately.

* Add Local Business schema markup (your site already has some schema; this extends it for local search and rich results).

* Clean up the smaller items as time allows: readable link URLs, remove inline styles, remove iFrames, hide the clear-text email address, and add an SPF mail record for deliverability.

3. **Confirm the fixes are live and indexed.**

* Once the developer reports back, open a browser tab in incognito mode and search “Elope Tofino” on Google. Confirm the new title and description show.

* In Google Search Console, confirm the sitemap is submitted and pages are being indexed; your Google Analytics and the Meta Pixel are already installed, so no tracking setup is needed here.

#### ***Part B: Publish Evergreen Resource and FAQ Pages***

4. **Add an FAQ page to your website.**

* List the real questions first-time couples ask: How long does it take to get to Tofino? Do we need a car? Is there Wi-Fi? What permits do you have? What’s included in an all-inclusive retreat? How much does an elopement cost?

* Keep answers short and direct. This format is exactly what AI tools pull from when someone asks about eloping in Tofino.

* Have your developer add it as its own page (not buried inside another) so it can be indexed and found.

5. **Plan a small library of evergreen resource pages.**

* Use ChatGPT to brainstorm: describe your business, then ask it to “interview me to come up with 10 resource-page ideas couples would Google before planning a Tofino elopement”. Answering its questions surfaces better ideas than letting it guess.

* Strong starting topics: “How to Elope in Tofino,” “The Best Secluded Spots for a Tofino Elopement,” “What a Day-Of Elopement Actually Looks Like,” “Eloping in Pacific Rim National Park,” and “Eloping in France with Elope Tofino.”

* Pick five; these live as standalone pages in a “Resources” or “Guides” section, not a dated blog, so they stay useful for years.

6. **Write and publish the resource pages with your coordinator.**

* Give your coordinator the topics, target keywords, and a clear instruction to write in your voice, using ChatGPT to outline rather than to write the final copy.

* End each page with one clear next step: a link to your contact or enquiry page.

* Publish roughly one page every two weeks through the plan; add each to the Resources section as it goes live.

#### ***Part C: Build Off-Site Authority***

7. **Claim your free local and review profiles.**

* Set up an Apple Business Profile (Apple Business Connect). It takes about ten minutes, done once, and most businesses skip it, so it’s easy visibility on Apple Maps.

* Claim a TripAdvisor profile; it’s a trusted source travellers check and it feeds your credibility and backlinks.

* Your Google Business Profile is already claimed. Review it to ensure that all fields are complete and accurate

8. **Get listed in free wedding directories.**

* Search (or ask ChatGPT) for current Canadian wedding directories: Wedlux, Today’s Bride, and Say I Do are active starting points.

* Claim every free listing; consider an affordable promoted listing (around $100) only where you can see real elopement traffic.

* Many couples still browse these, and the listings themselves are useful backlinks.

9. **Earn reciprocal links from the vendors you already work with.**

* List every vendor and venue you use regularly: photographers, celebrants, restaurants, rental companies, the Ucluelet accommodation.

* Reach out with a simple offer: you’ll feature them, and in return ask them to link to your relevant resource page (e.g. “How to Elope in Tofino”) from their site. A useful link beats a bare homepage link.

* Aim for 10–20 of these; they lift your search rankings and send qualified referral clicks.

## **Tactic 3: Social Content & Higher-Converting Ads** 

## **Phase:  Scale / Months 2–3**

**Sidebar Label:**  Social & Ads

**Subtitle:**  Give your coordinator a repeatable content system, replace weak ad creative with short video, and aim both at your all-inclusive retreats and France elopements.

### **What We Heard From You**

You have, in your words, “a ton” of professional photography sitting unused, and your current Instagram is just the occasional pretty photo with no intention behind it. Your Meta ads are run by a contractor who “literally takes a picture and says contact”. You feel you’re wasting money there. Meanwhile your real growth goals are specific: 10–15 all-inclusive retreat packages and 2–3 France elopements next year, plus leveraging the new Vancouver-to-Ucluelet floatplane. And you have a coordinator with a marketing background, who studied it at university, ready to take this on with direction from you.

### **What This Is**

This is a content and paid-reach system, built to be delegated. On the organic side, your Instagram does two jobs: showing what makes a Tofino elopement different (not generic wedding photos), and educating couples who don’t yet know a micro wedding is even an option through myth-busting and expert-tip posts that get forwarded to engaged friends. 

On video, short Reels made from clips your videographers already capture, because video reaches non-followers while photos mostly reach existing ones. On the paid side, we replace the “photo \+ contact” Meta ads with simple video ads and a warm call to action, and keep Google Ads disciplined rather than escalating the bidding war, all of it weighted toward your higher-value retreat and France offers.

### **Why It Matters**

This is where new couples discover you, and it’s the lever on revenue per wedding, not just wedding count, which is the growth you said you want. Educational content is what earns a follow and a share, turning your social into a referral channel rather than a gallery. Better ad creative means the money you already spend works harder, so you’re not forced deeper into the Google Ads auction. And pointing all of it at the all-inclusive retreats and France elopements is how you grow revenue 25% without doing 25% more weddings. It comes after the foundation work because the resource pages and review engine make every click that lands convert better.

### **Implementation Steps**

#### ***Part A: Build the Organic Content System***

1. **Set the two-layer Instagram strategy and brief your coordinator.**

* Layer one: posts that show what’s unique about a Tofino elopement: barefoot, in nature, in secluded park locations, not photos that could be from any wedding anywhere.

* Layer two: educational posts that build authority: “5 myths about elopements,” “What a micro wedding actually is,” “What couples forget to plan for”. The kind of posts that  a friend DMs to someone planning a wedding.

* Write a one-page guideline for your coordinator covering this split, and your nature-first, barefoot-not-ballroom brand voice.

2. **Generate a content backlog with ChatGPT.**

* Have your coordinator describe the business to ChatGPT and ask it to “interview me to come up with 10 educational posts for couples considering an elopement”. The interview approach produces far better ideas than asking it to write posts directly.

* Turn the best ideas into a batch of 10 posts designed in Canva, which you both already use.

* Schedule them in your existing (currently unused) Later account so posting doesn’t depend on finding a free moment.

3. **Capture and post short videos.**

* Brief your coordinator to pull her phone out at a fun moment on wedding days (with the couple’s permission, which your contract already covers) for short, candid clips.

* Ask your videographers, who already have footage on file, to cut a few short vertical Reels from existing weddings, and give the credit by tagging them in the posts.

* Post these as Reels and also share them to Stories; Reels reach people who don’t follow you yet, which is how the account will grow.

4. **Run a vendor-feature series.**

* Feature one vendor at a time: “Meet Paul, our photographer”, with editorial framing: ask each what they love about Tofino and what makes an elopement special, so the through-line is “eloping in Tofino is magical.”

* Post the feature, then send it to that vendor and ask them to link to it or reshare, feeding the reciprocal-link work in Tactic 2\.

* These double as content for the resource pages and give couples a feel for the people behind the experience.

#### ***Part B: Fix the Paid Ads***

5. **Replace the Meta ad creative with short video.**

* Direct your contractor (or coordinator) to build ads from four to five short video clips edited together with light music

* Lead with the experience: nature, barefoot, the secluded setting; end with a warm call to action like “Have you considered eloping in Tofino?” and a simple Contact button.

* Run it modestly at first and watch cost-per-result; you’re testing whether better creative lifts performance before committing more budget.

6. **Keep Google Ads disciplined and pointed at premium offers.**

* Ask your contractor to add ad copy for the all-inclusive retreat and France offers, which face less competition than generic “Tofino elopement.”

* Make sure ads point to the right page: the retreat or France resource page from Tactic 2, not just the homepage.

* Review with your contractor monthly: which keywords convert, what each booking costs, and where the higher-value packages are coming from.

#### ***Part C: Aim at Premium Growth***

7. **Build out the France and floatplane angles.**

* Move the France offer out of the pop-up and into your main navigation as a clear button (“Eloping in France?”), so the couples who want it can find it and the 99% who don’t aren’t interrupted.

* Add a short section or page referencing the new Vancouver-to-Ucluelet floatplane. When couples can fly in directly, no car needed, and post about it a few times a season.

* Post a couple of times each season about a France elopement you’ve done, planting seeds without building a whole campaign around it.

### **Tips**

* Don’t make social a second job. Reels cut from footage your videographers already shot, scheduled in batches, is most of the win.

* Educational posts are what get shared. A beautiful gallery gets a like; “5 myths about elopements” gets forwarded to someone actually planning one.

* Real, simple video beats polished ads. A candid clip with music outperforms a studio-style ad almost every time on Reels and in Meta ads.

* Point new attention at the high-value offers. Growing retreats and France is how revenue rises 25% without 25% more weddings.

* Always get the couple’s spoken okay before filming on the day, even though your contract covers it, it’s their moment first.
