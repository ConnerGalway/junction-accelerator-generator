## Tactic 1: Social Media Content System
Phase: Foundation
Short Label: Content System
Subtitle: Establish a weekly filming and posting rhythm that showcases the full Station experience

### What We Heard From You
You have a great camera, talented staff willing to participate, and more story than you're currently telling. The challenge isn't equipment or content — it's finding a sustainable rhythm that fits into your busy operations without burning anyone out.

### What This Is
A systematic approach to content creation built around four rotating pillars: The Station Experience, Food with Story, People & Staff, and Twin City Brewing. One hour of filming on Tuesdays, 30-45 minutes of editing mid-week, and 3+ posts per week.

### Why It Matters
Instagram is where travelers discover hidden gems on Highway 4. When someone searches "Port Alberni restaurant" or sees a friend share your reel, they need to see more than food photos — they need to feel the experience. Consistent, story-driven content builds that pull.

### Implementation Steps

#### Part A: Content Foundation
1. **Define your four content pillars**
   - Station Experience: atmosphere, service style, railway theme, patio, 24-tap wall
   - Food with Story: dishes with context about ingredients, chef's inspiration, pairings
   - People & Staff: server intros, kitchen glimpses, bartender picks, owner stories
   - Twin City Brewing: beer releases, brewing process, brewery tours, collaboration stories
   - Document these visibly in the kitchen or office for the team

2. **Set up your content calendar template**
   - Create a simple weekly grid (Tues filming → Wed/Thu editing → Fri/Sat/Sun posting)
   - Assign one pillar per week on rotation
   - Use a shared doc or whiteboard so everyone knows what's coming

3. **Prepare your filming kit**
   - Designate a charging spot for camera equipment
   - Create a "filming box" with tripod, ring light, and any props
   - Test audio setup for staff interviews (lapel mic or quiet corner)

#### Part B: Weekly Capture Rhythm
4. **Block Tuesday filming hour**
   - 5 min: Dining room B-roll (atmosphere, details, customers enjoying)
   - 10 min: Food capture (hero dish of the week, plating shots)
   - 10 min: Staff interview or behind-the-scenes moment
   - 5 min: Brewery footage (tap wall, brewing, beer pour)
   - Build buffer — not every week will be perfect

5. **Lower staff participation barriers**
   - Start with voiceover only (staff narrates while you film their hands)
   - Progress to short answers (one question, 10-second response)
   - Eventually build to longer interviews and on-camera comfort
   - Celebrate wins publicly to encourage others

#### Part C: Editing & Posting
6. **Schedule weekly editing time**
   - Block 30-45 minutes Wednesday or Thursday
   - Edit 3-4 reels/posts from Tuesday's footage
   - Batch-schedule using Meta Business Suite or Later
   - Don't aim for perfection — aim for consistency

7. **Optimize for discovery**
   - Create strong thumbnails (text overlay, expressive face, or beautiful food)
   - Hook viewers in first 1-3 seconds (start mid-action, ask a question, show the payoff)
   - Use 5-10 relevant hashtags (mix of local, food, travel, craft beer)
   - Add location tags for Port Alberni, Pacific Rim, Vancouver Island

8. **Post minimum 3x per week**
   - Ideal: 1 Reel, 1 carousel or static, 1 Story series
   - Best times: Test and track, but generally Thu-Sun for weekend planning
   - Cross-post to Facebook for broader local reach

| Metric | 30 Days | 60 Days | 90 Days |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Posts per week | 3+ | 3+ | 3+ |
| Reel views (average) | 500+ | 1,000+ | 2,000+ |

### Tips
- Imperfect content posted beats perfect content stuck in drafts
- Film more than you need — you can always cut, but can't add what you didn't capture
- Save your best-performing reels as templates to recreate
- Engage with comments within the first hour of posting for algorithm boost
- Repost user-generated content (with permission) to encourage more shares

## Tactic 2: Meta Ads Setup
Phase: Launch
Short Label: Meta Ads
Subtitle: Run targeted campaigns to drive reservations and build brand awareness with travelers

### What We Heard From You
You know Meta ads could work but haven't had the bandwidth to set them up properly. With ResOS tracking reservations and a clear weekend booking goal, you're well-positioned to run campaigns that actually tie back to results.

### What This Is
Two focused campaigns: one driving Friday/Saturday reservations to locals and lookalike audiences, and one building brand awareness with Vancouver Island travelers who might stop on Highway 4. Total budget around $25-30/day with clear tracking through ResOS.

### Why It Matters
Organic reach only goes so far. Paid ads let you reach travelers who've never heard of you — right when they're planning their trip. A $15/day campaign running Wed-Fri can fill tables that would otherwise sit empty.

### Implementation Steps

#### Part A: Pixel & Audience Setup
1. **Install the Meta Pixel**
   - Go to Meta Business Suite → Events Manager → Connect Data Sources
   - Select "Web" and create a new pixel named "Station Taphouse"
   - Install via WordPress plugin (PixelYourSite or Insert Headers and Footers)
   - Copy your Pixel ID and paste into the plugin settings
   - Verify it's firing with Meta Pixel Helper Chrome extension

2. **Build your core audiences**
   - Website Visitors (60 days): Everyone who's visited your site
   - Social Engagers (90 days): People who've interacted with your Instagram/Facebook
   - Customer List: Upload email list from ResOS if available (minimum 100 contacts)
   - Create these in Audiences section of Ads Manager

3. **Create Lookalike Audiences**
   - Based on Website Visitors or Customer List
   - Target: Canada or British Columbia
   - Size: 1% (most similar) or 2% (broader reach)
   - This is your secret weapon for finding new customers like your best ones

#### Part B: Campaign 1 — Reservation Driver
4. **Set up your Friday/Saturday campaign**
   - Objective: Sales (or Traffic if pixel isn't tracking conversions yet)
   - Campaign name: "Weekend Reservations — [Month]"
   - Budget: $15-20/day
   - Schedule: Wednesday through Friday only (catch weekend planners)
   - Placements: Advantage+ (let Meta optimize)

5. **Configure targeting**
   - Primary audience: 1% Lookalike based on website visitors
   - Geo-targeting: Vancouver Island + Lower Mainland (100km radius options)
   - Age: 25-65 (adjust based on your typical customer)
   - Interests: Optional layer of dining, craft beer, travel

6. **Create your ad**
   - Use your best-performing reel or a strong food/atmosphere video
   - Primary text: Clear value prop + call to action ("Book your table for this weekend")
   - Headline: "The Station Taphouse — Port Alberni"
   - Link: Direct to ResOS booking page
   - CTA button: "Book Now"

#### Part C: Campaign 2 — Brand Awareness
7. **Set up your traveler awareness campaign**
   - Objective: Reach or Brand Awareness
   - Campaign name: "Highway 4 Travelers — Always On"
   - Budget: $10/day
   - Schedule: Always running (continuous awareness)
   - Placements: Advantage+

8. **Target the traveler audience**
   - Geo-targeting: Vancouver Island
   - Interests: Tofino, Pacific Rim National Park, Cathedral Grove, BC road trips, Vancouver Island tourism
   - Age: 30-65
   - Exclude: Port Alberni (they already know you)

9. **Use story-driven creative**
   - Lead with your unique story: tsunami-origin brewery, railway heritage, 24-tap wall
   - Chef interviews, brewing process, "hidden gem" angle
   - Text: "Worth the stop on Highway 4" or "The best-kept secret between Nanaimo and Tofino"
   - Link to website homepage or Instagram profile

#### Part D: Tracking & Optimization
10. **Set up weekly tracking rhythm**
    - Every Monday: Check ResOS for weekend bookings, note any clustering
    - Train servers to ask "How did you hear about us?" — track responses
    - Log ad spend vs. reservation revenue in a simple spreadsheet

11. **Monthly optimization review**
    - First Monday of each month: Full ads review
    - Check: Cost per result, CTR (aim for 1%+), Frequency (refresh creative if >4)
    - Pause underperforming ads, duplicate winners with new creative
    - Test new audiences or creative angles

### Success Metrics
| Metric | 30 Days | 60 Days | 90 Days |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Pixel installed & firing | Yes | — | — |
| Campaign 1 live | Yes | Running | Optimized |
| Campaign 2 live | — | Yes | Running |
| Cost per result | Baseline | <$5 | <$3 |
| CTR | >0.5% | >1% | >1.5% |
| "Instagram/Facebook" referrals tracked | 5+ | 10+ | 15+ |

### Tips
- Start with Advantage+ placements — let Meta's algorithm find your audience before restricting
- Video outperforms static images 90% of the time for restaurants
- Refresh creative every 3-4 weeks to avoid ad fatigue
- Don't judge results until you've spent at least $50-100 on a campaign
- Retarget website visitors who didn't book — they're your warmest leads

## Tactic 3: Google Business Profile Optimization
Phase: Foundation
Short Label: Google & Reviews
Subtitle: Dominate local search and build a review engine that runs on autopilot

### What We Heard From You
You have two listings (The Station and Twin City Brewing) that could be working much harder. Google is often the first place travelers search when they're hungry on Highway 4, and reviews are the social proof that converts searchers into diners.

### What This Is
A complete audit and optimization of both Google Business Profiles, plus a systematic review-ask process that builds your reputation over time. Weekly posts keep your listing active, and events showcase what's happening at the brewery.

### Why It Matters
When someone searches "restaurants near me" in Port Alberni or "craft brewery Highway 4," you need to show up first — and look trustworthy. A listing with fresh photos, recent posts, and 200+ reviews beats a competitor with a bare-bones profile every time.

### Implementation Steps

#### Part A: Audit & Optimize Both Listings
1. **Access and verify both profiles**
   - Go to business.google.com and sign in
   - Claim both The Station Taphouse and Twin City Brewing if not already
   - Verify ownership via postcard, phone, or email if prompted

2. **Complete all business information**
   - Hours: Verify accuracy, including holiday hours
   - Phone: Ensure it's the main reservation line
   - Website: Link to homepage
   - Address: Confirm pin is in the right spot
   - Categories: Primary + secondary (Restaurant, Brewery, Pub, Craft Beer Bar)
   - Attributes: Add all relevant ones (outdoor seating, reservations, etc.)

3. **Upload 15+ quality photos per listing**
   - Exterior: Daytime and evening shots, signage, parking
   - Interior: Dining room, bar, tap wall, private areas
   - Food: 5-8 hero dishes, different angles
   - Drinks: Beer flights, signature pours, cocktails
   - People: Staff in action (with permission), happy customers
   - Front door: Helps customers find you

4. **Add menu and reservation links**
   - Upload current menu as photos or link to website menu
   - Add ResOS booking link in the "Reservations" section
   - Ensure ordering links (if applicable) are current

#### Part B: Weekly GBP Posts
5. **Schedule Monday posting routine**
   - Block 10 minutes every Monday
   - Create one post per listing (The Station + Twin City Brewing)
   - Rotate content types:
     - New menu items or features
     - Featured beer of the week
     - Upcoming events or live music
     - Holiday reminders and special hours
     - Media mentions or awards
   - Include photo, 50-100 words, CTA button ("Book" or "Learn More")

6. **Add GBP Events**
   - Brewery releases and tap takeovers
   - Special menus (holiday, pairing dinners)
   - Live music nights
   - Seasonal events
   - Publish 2-4 weeks in advance
   - Include photo and booking link

#### Part C: Review Generation System
7. **Set up your review infrastructure**
   - Get direct review link from GBP dashboard (Marketing → Get more reviews)
   - Create QR code linking to review page (use Canva or free QR generator)
   - Design table cards: "Loved your visit? A Google review means the world to us"
   - Print 10-20 cards for tables and check presenters

8. **Train staff on review asks**
   - Identify the right moment: after a compliment, positive feedback, or "best meal" comment
   - Script: "That means so much! If you have a moment, a Google review would really help us out."
   - Hand them the card or mention the QR code
   - Never offer discounts or incentives for reviews (against Google policy)

9. **Add review links to digital touchpoints**
   - Post-visit email from ResOS (if available)
   - Email signature for owner/manager
   - Link in Instagram bio or Stories
   - QR code on printed receipts

10. **Respond to ALL reviews**
    - Within 48 hours, respond to every review
    - Positive: Thank them specifically, mention something from their review
    - Negative: Apologize, take it offline ("Please email us at..."), show you care
    - Responses show future customers you're engaged

### Success Metrics
| Metric | 30 Days | 60 Days | 90 Days |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| Both profiles fully optimized | Yes | — | — |
| Photos uploaded | 15+ each | 20+ each | 25+ each |
| Weekly posts published | 4/week | 8/week | 12/week |
| New reviews (combined) | 15+ | 35+ | 50+ |
| Review response rate | 100% | 100% | 100% |
| Average rating maintained | 4.5+ | 4.5+ | 4.5+ |

### Tips
- Photos with people get 42% more engagement than empty room shots
- Posts with offers or events get more clicks than generic updates
- Respond to negative reviews gracefully — future customers read your responses
- Ask for reviews when the experience is fresh (same day > next week)
- Twin City Brewing profile can link to Station for "food at our partner restaurant"
